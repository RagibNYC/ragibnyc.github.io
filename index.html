Project Background
The problem: Premium Merchant Funding had no structured pipeline tracking system. Deal stages were tracked in scattered notes, there was zero visibility into budget vs. actuals, and the client qualification process was inconsistent. I was handed a blank slate and told to close deals. What I built: A full Excel-based pipeline operations system with XLOOKUP-driven scenario modeling, rolling budget vs. actuals tracking, and a repeatable client qualification workflow — then used it to close $200,000+ in funded capital across 10+ clients in 8 weeks.

Methodology
Tools used: Advanced Excel (XLOOKUP, dynamic arrays, scenario manager), manual CRM tracking, financial modeling frameworks.

Process: (1) Conducted structured financial discovery calls with SMB owners to collect cash flow statements, revenue history, and liability data. (2) Built creditworthiness models to score each client and match them to the optimal funding product. (3) Created a rolling pipeline tracker in Excel that monitored deal stages, funding amounts, and budget vs. actuals weekly. (4) Used scenario modeling to project monthly revenue under conservative, base, and optimistic deal-close assumptions. (5) Redesigned the client qualification workflow into a repeatable 3-step process to reduce deal cycle friction.

Project Background
The problem: ProACE's financial reporting was entirely manual — analysts pulled data from 4 separate source systems into Excel each week, reconciled by hand, and built reports from scratch. Data accuracy sat at ~67% due to human error in reconciliation. Reporting cycle time was 5 days per report. What I built: An end-to-end SQL ETL pipeline (joins, CTEs, aggregations) to automate extraction and validation, automated Power BI dashboards with DAX time intelligence (CALCULATE, SUMX, YTD/MTD/rolling), and leveraged Claude AI to identify further workflow bottlenecks and automate report summarization — achieving 100% accuracy and 1-day cycle times.
